Guwahati, formerly known as Pragjyotishpura, is the largest city in the Indian state of Assam and the largest urban area in Northeast India. Situated on the south bank of the Brahmaputra, it serves as the crucial link for trade and tourism to the entire region. The city is famous for its ancient temples, notably the Kamakhya Temple, and its scenic landscapes.

It's a vibrant hub blending commercial activity with rich cultural heritage, often referred to as the 'City of Temples'.
