Local Traditions & Festivals

Bihu Festivals

The most important festival, celebrated three times a year. Rongali Bihu (mid-April) marks the Assamese New Year and is celebrated with traditional dances and songs. The Bihu Dance is the heart of Assamese culture.

Ambubachi Mela

A major annual gathering held at the Kamakhya Temple during the monsoon season. It draws millions of pilgrims and Sadhus from across India, making it a spectacular display of faith and tradition.

Assamese Dress

The traditional attire, the Mekhela Chadar for women, and the Dhoti-Gamosa for men, are frequently worn during festivals and special occasions, showcasing the fine silk and cotton weaving of the region.

Famous Dishes

Assamese Thali

A complete meal featuring rice, various types of 'dal' (lentils), at least two vegetable preparations, and a non-vegetarian curry like **'Maas tenga' (fish curry)** or duck meat, often cooked with tangy components.

Khar

A signature dish of Assam. It's a class of dishes prepared with the main ingredient being an alkaline liquid filtered from burnt banana peels. It's often eaten at the start of a meal.

Pitha

Rice cakes or crepes, often sweet or savory, prepared especially during Bihu. Varieties include 'Til Pitha' (sesame) and 'Narikol Pitha' (coconut).
